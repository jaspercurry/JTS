## Executive summary

This codebase has **very good bones and an accumulating drift tax**. The architecture discipline is real and held up under a line-by-line adversarial read: the extensibility doctrine (registries + protocol seams + single-writer reconcilers) produced only **5 boundary violations across 1,506 files**, safety ceilings are pinned by guard tests, privilege separation is genuinely engineered, and there are **zero credentials in the tree**. A second contributor could trust the *structure* everywhere.

What they could not yet trust is the *periphery*: **1 fabricated doc section + 5 major-drift docs** (including SECURITY.md misstating the control API's bind surface), **93 confirmed dead-code findings** (including a 764-line module with no caller), **169 mapped duplication clusters** (atomic-write hand-rolled 13×, a JSON escaper 7× across 4 Rust crates), a **29-file monster corridor** carrying most of the complexity, and **42 dead/renamed env flags** in docs. None of this is rot — it is single-maintainer velocity outrunning hygiene, and all of it is now mapped to six executable fix waves.

The completeness critic (below, verbatim) validated reader quality 3/3 on independent spot-reads but caught the audit's own blind spots honestly: two real cross-tile bugs were rationalised past (both independently fixed on main the same day), and the audit ran on a fork that main has already moved under. **Read every finding with the rebase-first rule: verify against current main before fixing.** 8 findings are already mooted by main's deletions; ~7 of main's 14 new commits fixed audit-adjacent bugs.
